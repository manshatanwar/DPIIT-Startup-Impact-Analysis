Strategic Impact Analysis of India’s Startup Ecosystem (2020–2026)
A Data-Driven Study on Recognition Trends and Employment Generation
Project Overview:
This project evaluates the socio-economic impact of the Startup India initiative by analyzing official datasets from the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT). As a BMS student minoring in Data Engineering, I developed this study to identify how government recognition translates into national employment.

 Key Insights:
The 2025 Inflection Point: Identified an exponential surge in job creation starting in late 2024, signaling the ecosystem's transition from incubation to high-scale maturity.

Efficiency Hubs: Engineered a "Job Density" KPI which revealed that regional startups in states like Nagaland generate significantly higher employment per entity (32+ jobs) compared to urban tech hubs.

Sectoral Diversification: Mapped the shift from IT Services (19.8%) toward strategic sectors like Healthcare & Lifesciences (16.2%) and Renewable Energy (5.2%).

Technical Stack:
Language: Python

Libraries: Pandas (Data Wrangling), Matplotlib & Seaborn (Statistical Visualization)

Techniques:  Multi-key Merging, and Sectoral Aggregation.

Dataset Sources:
Industry-wise Startup Recognition (2024): Sourced from Open Government Data (OGD) Platform India.

State-wise Direct Job Creation (2020-2025): Sourced from Open Government Data (OGD) Platform India
